Function?

INKJET: An inkjet printer's primary function is to print physical copies of digital files. A driver is a type of specialised software that makes this conversion feasible.Â The driver translates the digital text or picture file into a language that the printer can read. The print head, a part that holds the ink cartridges, receives this information after that. Thousands of small holes are present in the print head. The cartridge reservoirs' ink is then pressurised and driven through the holes to produce large amounts of distinct ink droplets. To form the image, the printhead moves quickly across the page, dispersing tiny ink droplets. This is how the inkjet printer works.

LASER: In order to positively charge a drum, a heated wire is used in laser printers. The drum is then passed over by a laser, which reverses the charge in the areas it strikes. The image or text to be printed is represented by the now negatively charged regions of the drum. After that, the drum is run over by a toner roller, and the negatively charged regions of the drum attract toner particles. To create a printer copy of a digital document or image, a sheet of paper is then fed underneath the toner-coated drum and the toner is passed onto its surface. Due to its reduced tendency to smear compared to inkjet printing, laser printing has established itself as a dependable and efficient printing alternative.
